Shenyang Palace Museum
SunLiu
2024-11-10

The Shenyang Palace Museum is located in Shenyang, Liaoning Province, China. It is one of the two complete imperial palace architectural complexes in China, second only to the Forbidden City in Beijing. It was first built in the early Qing Dynasty (1625) by Emperor Taizu Nurhaci and Emperor Taizong Huang Taiji, and was used as a royal palace before the Qing Dynasty entered the country. The Shenyang Forbidden City covers an area of approximately 60000 square meters, with an architectural style that combines Manchu characteristics and is deeply influenced by traditional Han architecture, possessing unique artistic and historical value.

The Shenyang Palace Museum is divided into three parts:
  • Middle Road: including the main buildings such as the Grand Hall and the Chongzheng Hall, it is the place where the emperor holds court meetings and offices.
  • Donglu: Built during the early Nurhaci period, it was mainly used for worship, including the Qingning Palace.
  • West Road: mainly used for religious activities, with buildings such as Phoenix Tower.
